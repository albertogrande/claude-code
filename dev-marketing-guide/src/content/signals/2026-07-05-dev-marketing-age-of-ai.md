Helen Min went back to her 2020 advice on building developer brands and, with
Matt Palmer (developer relations at Replit), rewrote it for the AI era. The
piece is the clearest public statement so far of a shift practitioners have
been feeling all year.

What's different, in their telling: the audience now includes semi-technical
builders shipping production apps with AI assistance; AI assistants consume
your documentation alongside humans, so technical resources need to be
LLM-readable; demo cycles compressed from weeks to days, so the noise floor
rose; and community moved from Stack Overflow and forums to Discord, Slack,
and AI-powered support. Their speed bar is quotable: "if your quickstart
takes more than a few minutes, you've already lost."

What's unchanged is the list most dev marketers already know: show don't
tell, features over benefits, genuinely help developers, be direct, remove
friction. Min's closing argument is the interesting part — as AI converges
technical capabilities, "taste becomes the moat": point of view and developer
experience become the differentiators, Stripe being her example of a brand
whose opinionated taste does competitive work.
