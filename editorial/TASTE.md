# Reader Taste

Agent-maintained. Durable preferences only — update when the reader gives
feedback (in session or via issue comments); prune one-off reactions.

## Who the reader is

A **heavy daily Claude Code user working almost entirely in the macOS and iOS
apps** — not the terminal. Power user, not beginner. Uses subagents,
workflows, skills, MCP, hooks; cares about context budget and token spend.
Wants the state of the art, kept honest.

## What the reader wants

- **Practical and testable.** A concrete habit, setting, or workflow they can
  put into practice today — "clear your context after…", "switch to Fable
  when…", "cap a workflow at N agents…". A tip they can try beats an
  observation.
- **Two jobs, not one.** (1) Stay current each week — the short weekly digest.
  (2) An always-current guide covering the whole corpus. Signals feed both;
  don't confuse the two.
- **Opinionated.** State a take in one plain sentence. Make calls. Hedge only
  when honestly uncertain, and say why.
- **Sourced.** Every claim traceable to a primary source (docs, changelog,
  release notes) or a linked community thread. Distinguish shipped features
  from research previews.

## Voice

- Clarity above all: short sentences, simple words, short paragraphs. Depth
  from specifics — model IDs, version numbers, exact settings — not from
  rhetorical flourish. If a sentence needs reading twice, rewrite it.
- Oriented to the apps (macOS/iOS), not the terminal. Frame every tip for how
  it plays on the desktop and phone.
- Inherited house style from The Wire: what happened → why it matters → what
  to do about it.

## What bores the reader

- Padding a quiet day into a forced daily article — the reason we moved to
  signals + a weekly digest. A quiet stretch is fine; capture it and move on.
- Narrow, samey "how X works" explainers with no practical payoff.
- Too technical for the sake of it — the reader wants leverage, not trivia.

## Format

- **Signals** (daily, internal) — raw one-liners, capture over synthesis.
- **The guide** (evergreen) — the product; kept continuously current.
- **The weekly** (one short essay a week) — decides what mattered; beats/areas
  are research lenses, never output sections. Not a per-item news list.
- **Deep dives** — commissioned only when a thread earns it (see MEMORY.md),
  or on demand by topic.
